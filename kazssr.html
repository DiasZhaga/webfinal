KazSSR:

Kazakhstan under the rule of the Soviets.

State symbols

The official flag of the KazSSR

The new flag of the Kazakh SSR was approved by the Decree of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the Kazakh SSR on January 24, 1953. It was a red cloth with a golden hammer and sickle, as well as a red star framed with a gold border, in the upper corner at the shaft; in the lower part of the cloth there was a blue horizontal stripe, the width of the blue stripe was equal to 2/9 of the width of the flag, and the distance to it from the lower edge of the flag was 1/9 of the width of the flag. The ratio of the length of the flag to the width is 2:1.

Coat of arms of the Kazakh SSR

The Kazakh SSR included the following regions: Semipalatinsk - Pavlodar, Semipalatinsk, Ust-Kamenogorsk, Zaisan and Karkarala counties. Akmola - Atbasarsky, Akmola, Kokchetavsky, Petropavlovsk and part of Omsk county. Turgayskaya - Kustanaysky, Aktobe, Irgizsky and Turgaysky counties.

Creation of KazSSR and First Constitution

On December 5, 1936, the Kazakh Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic (KASSR), formed in 1920, was transformed into the Union Republic of the USSR. In this regard, on March 24, 1937, the Extraordinary X Congress of Soviets of Kazakhstan adopted the first Constitution of the Kazakh SSR.

The first Constitution of the Kazakh SSR was built in full accordance with the Constitution of the USSR, consisted of 11 chapters and 125 articles. It reflected the creation of the foundations of socialism in the republic, the establishment of public ownership of the means of production and the planned economy, the elimination of exploitation and the formation of equal relations. The Constitution of the Kazakh SSR of 1937 legislatively consolidated the unification of Kazakhstan with other republics of the USSR in order to provide mutual assistance in the line of economic and political defense.

The first decade of Kazakh art and literature in Moscow

A landmark event in the history of Kazakh culture was the First Decade of Kazakh art and literature in Moscow. Evgeny Brusilovsky's operas based on folk music - "Kyz-Zhibek" and "Zhalbyr" were shown on the stage of Moscow theaters. The performance was staged by a young team of the Kazakh Musical Theater. The first national theater in Kazakhstan was founded just 10 years earlier - in 1926, and until 1934 the opera soloists performed on the same stage with dramatic actors. Moscow journalists wrote rave reviews about the premiere of "Kyz-Zhibek".

Deportation of peoples

In the mid-30s, one of the forms of political repression against social and ethnic groups became deportations - the forced eviction of a large mass of people to a remote and unfamiliar area. With the approach of the war, entire peoples who, according to the country's leadership, were considered unreliable began to be deported. One of the main places of resettlement of the deportees was the territory of the Kazakh SSR.

In 1936, 15,000 Polish and German families were evicted from the border zone of Ukraine to Kazakhstan. A year later, more than 95 thousand Koreans from the Far East were sent to Kazakhstan. On August 28, 1941, after the liquidation of the Autonomous Republic of the Volga Germans, the largest deportation action was launched. In six months, more than 1 million 30 thousand Germans were resettled from all over the USSR to remote areas of Siberia and Kazakhstan (445 thousand of them settled in the Kazakh SSR). In the autumn of 1943, operations for the resettlement of the North Caucasian peoples began. Initially, Karachay residents were resettled, and in February 1944, during Operation Lentil, 496,000 Chechens and Ingush were deported to the Kazakh SSR and the Kyrgyz SSR in 15 days. In subsequent years, Crimean Tatars, Greeks, Balkars, Meskhetian Turks, and Kurds were also deported to Kazakhstan.

Political repression in Kazakhstan

During the period of repression in 1937-1938, more than 100 thousand people were repressed in the republic.

On April 26, 1937, an article "On the occasion of bourgeois nationalists" was published in the All-Union newspaper Pravda. Its author, journalist Pukhov, actually accused the leadership of the Kazakh SSR in the absence of mass arrests of "counter-revolutionary nationalists." The article intensified the growth of suspicion and distrust and became the impetus for the launch of a mechanism of mass repression in Kazakhstan. A few months before that, the directive of the NKVD of the USSR "On intelligence and operational work on anti-Soviet Turkic-Tatar nationalist organizations" was issued, which affected all the eastern republics of the USSR. The peak of arrests in the Kazakh SSR occurred in October-December 1937. The punitive authorities first of all dealt with their long-time opponents - the Alashordins. In 1937-1938, A. Bukeikhanov, A. Baitursynov, M. Zhumabaev, M. Tynyshpaev, brothers Zhansha and Khalel Dosmukhamedov and other figures of the Alash autonomy were arrested and shot.

Their opponents also fell under the wave of repression. Fighters for the establishment of Soviet power and prominent party figures were declared enemies of the people: People's Commissar of Education of the Kazakh SSR T. Zhurgenov, Deputy Chairman of the SNK of the RSFSR (Deputy Prime Minister) T. Ryskulov, Deputy Chairman of the Kazakh Academy of Sciences S. Asfendiyarov, head of the Press Department of the Central Committee of the CP(b) of Kazakhstan A. Rozybakiev. The founders of Kazakh Soviet literature S. Seifullin, B. Mailin, I. Zhansugurov also fell under the ice rink of repression. The First Secretary of Kazakhstan Levon Mirzoyan did not escape arrest and execution either. In addition to the country's leadership and representatives of the national intelligentsia, thousands of civil servants and public figures were arrested. Several public trials were held in the Kazakh SSR, including the Karaganda case, in which 400 people were repressed. In two years, more than 100 thousand people were repressed in the republic. About 25 thousand of them were shot.

Creation of the Academy of Sciences of the Kazakh SSR

On June 1, 1946, at a solemn meeting in the auditorium of the Opera and Ballet Theater, in the presence of the most famous scientists of the country and the republic, the Academy of Sciences of the Kazakh SSR was opened. It was established on the basis of the Kazakh branch of the USSR Academy of Sciences (until 1938 - the Kazakh base of the USSR Academy of Sciences). Until the 40s, 11 research and design institutes already worked in Kazakhstan, but there was a shortage of personnel: out of 100 employees of the Kazakh branch of the USSR Academy of Sciences, only three had a doctorate degree, 14 were candidates of sciences. Kazakh scientists conducted research in the field of biology, history and ethnography, mineral exploration.

Independence of the Republic of Kazakhstan

October 25, 1990 is the day of sovereignty, which became a turning point in Kazakhstan's History. The first fundamental legislative act of the country was the Declaration of Independence. It is a legal, political and ideological document reflecting the basic principles of the public, human rights and freedoms, property, political pluralism and separation of powers. These fundamental provisions formed the basis of the Constitution.

On December 16, 1991, Kazakhstan adopted the Constitutional Law on Independence in the Republic of Kazakhstan.
