Republic of Kazakhstan

The Republic of Kazakhstan is a unitary state with the presidential system of government. Under the Constitution, Kazakhstan is a democratic, secular, legal and social state which recognizes the man, his life, rights and freedoms as the supreme values of the country.

Kazakhstan gained independence on December 16, 1991. Astana is the capital city of the country. Kazakh language is the official language of Kazakhstan. The Russian language has the status of the language of interethnic communication. The unit of currency used in Kazakhstan is tenge.

Kazakh khanate

The Kazakh Khanate, in eastern sources known as Ulus of the Kazakhs, Ulus of Jochi, Yurt of Urus,
was a Kazakh state in Central Asia, successor of the Golden Horde existing
from the 15th to the 19th century, centered on the eastern parts of the Desht-i Qipchaq.

The Kazakh Khanate existed for several centuries, and during this time,
there were numerous engagements with neighboring powers, internal struggles, and conflicts.
However, at the beginning of the XVIII century,
the situation of the state was difficult and the then Khan Abulkhair had to go to extreme measures.

The beginning of Kazakhstan's accession to Russia.

Khan Abulkhair realized that the only real force capable of protecting the khanate torn by contradictions from external danger at that time was the Russian Empire. She has achieved notable success in international affairs. Turning to the Russian state, Abulkhair hoped to suspend the attack on the Younger Zhuz of the Volga Kalmyks and Bashkirs - subjects of Russia - and thus secure the north-western borders of the Kazakh Khanate from neighbors. There were motives of personal interest of the ruler of the Younger Zhuz in accepting Russian citizenship. Abulkhair, addressing Russia, in addition to the circumstances noted, sought to oust his opponents from the political arena, weaken them and rise above other Genghisids, relying on Russia. The main task of the Younger Zhuz Khan was to establish trusting relations with neighboring Russia and organize all forces to repel the Dzungarian aggression.

The embassy sent by Abulkhair was accepted by Anna Ioannovna, and on February 19, 1731, she signed a decree on the admission of the Younger Zhuz to the citizenship of the empire. By order of the Empress, an embassy headed by the translator of the Board of Foreign Affairs A. Tevkelev was sent to the Kazakh Khanate to sign a certificate of Russian citizenship by the Kazakh Khan and his entourage.

The collapse of the empire

After the fall of the imperial power in 1917, the Kazakh intelligentsia tried with all its might to gain sovereignty, it was from this moment until 1991 that Kazakhstan began its path to independence. During this time, Kazakhstan has gone from the autonomy of the RSFSR to a full-fledged member of the Soviet Union.

Heroes of Independence

Akhmet Baitursynuly

Kazakh educator, teacher, publicist, translator, Turkologist, linguist and politician. One of the leaders and active figures of the Alash movement.

More Info
Prominent public figure and politician who took an active part in the national liberation movement in Kazakhstan: member of the Provisional People's Council of Alash-Orda (1917-1920), editor-in-chief of the socio-political newspaper "Kazakh", published in 1913-1918. The first People's Commissar of Education of the Kazakh ASSR (1920-1921). The creator of the so-called "new spelling" (kaz. zhana emle), also known as the "Baitursynov" Arabic alphabet for the Kazakh language. At the beginning of the XXI century, this alphabet was used by Kazakhs in China, Afghanistan, Iran, Turkey and some other countries.

Alikhan Bukeikhanov

Kazakh statesman, political and public figure, teacher, journalist and ethnographer. Founder and one of the leaders of the Alash Party. Chairman of the government of the Alash autonomy (Kazakh national territorial entity) from 1917 to 1920.

More Info
Participated in various meetings to organize the Kazakh branch of the Cadet Party. After the February revolution, Bukeikhanov took an active part in the national liberation struggle of the Kazakh people. After two all-Kazakhstan congresses held in July and December 1917, it was possible to create the Alash party and the Alashorda government, Bukeikhanov was elected its chairman. The name of the party was proposed by the delegate of the Ural region, lawyer Zhanna Dosmukhamedov, and was supported by everyone. The newspaper "Kazakh" became the printing organ of the party. It was a truly historic event, as the creation of a unified Kazakh state became a reality for the first time.

Mirzhakip Dulatov

Kazakh poet, writer, journalist, playwright, one of the leaders of the Alash-Orda government and the national liberation movement of Kazakhstan. He is also known under the pseudonyms Madiyar, Azamat, Timiner, Turk Balasy and Argyn Balasy.

More Info
The first poem by Dulatov was published in the first issue of the Kazakh newspaper Serke (only 2 issues were published) in St. Petersburg. The first collection of poems “Oyan, kazak!” (“Wake up, Kazakh!”) was released in Kazan in 1909. The title of the book served as a manifesto, an appeal of the Kazakh intelligentsia at the beginning of the 20th century to the people. The entire print run of the book was destroyed, and the author was persecuted and imprisoned. However, Dulatov actively continued his literary and journalistic activities. During this period , he publishes the novel “The Unfortunate Zhamal" (“Bakytsyz Zhamal", Orenburg, 1910), a collection of works “Azamat" (Orenburg, 1913), “Terme" (Orenburg, 1915). In his works, influencing the minds and hearts of fellow citizens, he draws attention to the responsibility of each individual to the people, advocates for science, education, equality of women. “The Unfortunate Zhamal" is the first novel in Kazakh literature, was a huge success and made the author famous.
