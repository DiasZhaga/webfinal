In 1932, the Cossack ASSR bordered the Lower Volga Region in the west, the Middle Volga Region in the northwest, the Ural Region in the north, the West Siberian Region in the northeast, the Kyrgyz ASSR and the Soviet Republics of Central Asia in the south, and China in the southeast.

In February 1925, the Orenburg province and the city of Orenburg (the capital of the Kyrgyz Territory since July 10, 1919, the capital of the Cossack ASSR from August 26, 1920 to February 1925) were withdrawn from the Cossack ASSR of the RSFSR into the RSFSR proper (directly subordinate to the RSFSR).

Creation of KazASSR

The Civil War in Russia was still going on, and the Bolsheviks had already begun to create the first socialist state. Immediately after the overthrow of the Provisional Government, Vladimir Lenin announced the creation of the Soviet Federative Socialist Republic. Kazakh Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic was formed as part of the RSFSR. Naturally, this process was not instantaneous. Two years later, on July 10, 1919 the Decree on the Revolutionary Committee for the Administration of the Kyrgyz (Kazakh) Region was published. The power of the Revkom extended to the territories where the Kazakh population lived. These are the Ural, Turgai, Akmola and Semipalatinsk regions, as well as a number of territories of the Astrakhan province. On August 26, 1920, the Soviet government issued a decree signed by Mikhail Kalinin and Vladimir Lenin "On the formation of an Autonomous Kyrgyz (Kazakh) Soviet Socialist Republic" as part of the RSFSR. The capital of the Kazakh autonomy was the city of Orenburg.

The birthday of the Kazakh Soviet national statehood.

On October 4, 1920, the Founding Congress of the KazASSR Soviets was held in Orenburg. This day is the birthday of the Kazakh Soviet national statehood. At the Congress, the Central Executive Committee (CEC) was elected by the authorities in the autonomy headed by S. Mendeshev, the SNK (Council of People's Commissars) headed by V. Radus-Zenkovich. The "Declaration of Workers' Rights of KazASSR" was adopted at the congress. The Declaration served as a constitution. It secured broad political rights for the workers of Kazakhstan.

The Transformation of Territory and Identity

However, the territorial consolidation of the Kazakh people with the formation of KazASSR was not completed. Semirechenskaya and Syrdarya regions were part of the Turkestan ASSR, and Semipalatinsk and Akmola regions were under the jurisdiction of the Sibrevkom. The question of the actual annexation of these lands to Kazakhstan was finally resolved later. In October 1924, the demarcation of the Soviet republics in Central Asia was carried out. As a result of the delimitation of the borders, the Syrdarya and Semirechenskaya regions became part of the KazASSR. The territory of Kazakhstan has increased and amounted to 2.7 million square kilometers. The population has reached 5230 thousand. 61% of them were Kazakhs. In April 1925, the Ak Mosque became the capital of the autonomy, renamed Kyzylorda. In addition, in April 1925, the historically accurate name of the Kazakh people was restored. Recall that during the colonial period, Kazakhs were mistakenly called Kyrgyz, and as a result, the autonomy of the Kazakhs was originally called the Kyrgyz ASSR.

KazASSR: results

Thus, in 1920, the Kazakh ASSR was created as part of the RSFSR. The proclamation of the Kazakh Soviet national statehood had great historical significance. The Kazakh people perceived this act as the result of a centuries-old struggle against colonialism, the first step on the path of national revival. In 1936, the status of the republic increased. According to the new Constitution of the USSR, the Kazakh ASSR was transformed into the Kazakh SSR. Despite all the rights declared in the new Union Constitution, Kazakhstan has not received true independence and even self-government. And yet, even if the formal recognition of the sovereignty of Kazakhstan and the legislative consolidation of its territory played a huge role in the history of the state.
